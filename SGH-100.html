SGH-100

The SGH-T100 was one the world’s first TFT-LCD Color mobile phones made in 2002. It was also the first mobile phone to use a thin-film transistor active matrix LCD display The chairman of Samsung named Lee was involved in the development process which led to the phone be known as the “Lee Kun - Hee Phone”. The phone won multiple awards due to its design and the design of their other products. The picture above shows the thin design which many people liked. They also really enjoyed the fact the phone had color to it unlike the previous phones Samsung, (such as the SPH-M2500 ,The worlds first MP3 phone, and the SH-100 South Korea’s first mobile phone) which made it a big hit internationally in places all around Asia and Europe. Due to the phone being so important it was the first mobile phone to sell for than 10 million phones within the year it had been relaunched.
